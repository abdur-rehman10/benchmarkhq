151 Features Every News Website Should Have: The Global Benchmark

April 2, 2026 · 6 min read

We're conducting the largest news website benchmark ever — scanning 1,730 news sites across 147 countries against a 151-feature checklist covering everything from article typography to paywall strategy to accessibility compliance.

What We Check

The 151 features span 16 categories:

  • Article Experience (18) — headlines, bylines, reading time, typography, related articles
  • Navigation (15) — hero area, section nav, trending, breaking news, archive
  • Monetization (12) — paywall type, subscription tiers, donations, micropayments
  • Multimedia (10) — video, live streaming, podcasts, data journalism
  • Accessibility (10) — semantic HTML, contrast, keyboard nav, screen reader support
  • Trust & Transparency (10) — about page, editorial policy, corrections, author profiles

Early Findings

Premium outlets (NYT, FT, Guardian) score highest on accessibility and trust signals. Ad-supported sites score highest on engagement features but lowest on ad experience quality. Public broadcasters (BBC, ABC, CBC) lead in accessibility compliance across every market.
